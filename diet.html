What Do Rabbits Eat?

Rabbits are herbivores, primarily feeding on a high-fiber diet that consists of:

1. Hay

2. Fresh Water

Hay: The cornerstone of a rabbit's diet, hay (especially timothy hay) provides essential fiber that aids digestion and helps wear down their continuously growing teeth.

Hay is the most important part of a rabbit's diet, providing essential fiber that supports healthy digestion and prevents gastrointestinal issues. The high fiber content also helps wear down their constantly growing teeth, reducing the risk of dental problems.

Fresh Water: Clean, fresh water should always be available, as hydration is crucial for their overall health.

Fresh, clean water is essential for rabbits' health. It should be available at all times, either in a heavy, spill-proof bowl or a water bottle with a sipper tube. Regularly check and refill their water to ensure hydration, which is crucial for digestion and overall well-being
