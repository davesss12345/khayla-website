Where Rabbits Live

Rabbits are versatile animals that can thrive in a variety of habitats, each offering different resources and shelter. Their adaptability allows them to occupy diverse environments, including:

1. Forests

2. Grasslands and Meadows

1. Forests: Many rabbit species, such as the Eastern Cottontail, can be found in wooded areas. These habitats provide ample cover from predators, with underbrush and fallen leaves offering places to hide and nest.

Grasslands and Meadows: Open fields and grasslands are ideal for rabbits, as they provide abundant grasses and herbs for feeding. The open spaces allow rabbits to see potential threats from a distance, while clumps of grass or shrubs offer necessary shelter.
